01 WHAT IS A PCB?

A Printed Circuit Board (PCB) is a flat board made of insulating material (usually FR4 — fibreglass) with conductive copper traces etched onto it to electrically connect components.

Think of it as a permanent, miniaturized version of your breadboard — but instead of jumper wires, copper is chemically bonded to the board and etched into precise pathways.

For robotics: PCBs let you ditch the mess of wires, make your robot more reliable, lighter, more compact, and ultimately professional-grade.

03 TRACE RULES & SPECS
〰️
SIGNAL TRACE WIDTH
0.2 mm
Minimum for low-current signals (SPI, I²C, GPIO). Use a trace width calculator for power.
⚡
POWER TRACE WIDTH
0.5–2mm
Wider = lower resistance = less heat. 1A needs ≈ 0.3mm on outer layer (1oz copper).
📏
CLEARANCE (MIN)
0.2 mm
Minimum gap between any two copper elements. Prevents short circuits and arcing.
🔩
VIA DRILL
0.2–1mm
Hole through board connecting layers. Smaller vias cost more. 0.3mm is common budget standard.
🟫
COPPER WEIGHT
1 oz / ft²
Standard is 1oz (35μm). 2oz for high-current boards. Heavier = more ampacity.
📐
TRACE ANGLES
45° only
Never use 90° corners — they cause signal reflections at high frequencies. Always 45° curves.

04 COMPONENT PACKAGES
SMD
THROUGH-HOLE
CONNECTORS

Surface Mount Devices — soldered directly to pads on the board surface. Smaller, better for RF, cheaper in production. Harder to hand-solder.

▬
RESISTOR / CAPACITOR
0402 / 0603 / 0805
0402 = 1mm×0.5mm (tweezers needed). 0805 is beginner-friendly. Named by size in hundredths of inches.
▲
SOT-23 TRANSISTOR
SOT-23-3
Small 3-pin package. Common for MOSFETs and BJTs. Legs at 0.95mm pitch. Solderable by hand.
◼
SOIC / TSSOP IC
SOIC-8 / TSSOP-16
8–16 pin ICs. 1.27mm or 0.65mm pitch. Motor drivers, op-amps, MCUs. Soldarable with flux.
⬛
QFP / QFN IC
QFP-48 / QFN-32
High pin-count MCUs. QFN has exposed pad underneath (heat sink). Requires reflow oven or hot air.
🟫
INDUCTOR
1210 / 1008
Used in power supplies (buck/boost converters). Larger packages handle more current.
💡
LED (SMD)
0603 / 5050
0603 for indicator LEDs. 5050 (WS2812B) for addressable RGB strips and robot lighting.

Through-hole components have leads that pass through drilled holes. Mechanically stronger, easier to hand-solder, but takes up more board space.

🟡
DIP IC
DIP-8 / DIP-28
Classic IC package. 2.54mm pin pitch — breadboard compatible. Great for prototyping (ATmega328 = DIP-28).
🔵
ELECTROLYTIC CAP
Radial, various
Larger capacitors for power filtering. Polarized — always mind the + and – pins!
🔴
LED (TH)
3mm / 5mm
Standard indicator LED. Long leg = anode (+). Needs current-limiting resistor always.
🔌
BARREL JACK
DC-005 / PJ-002A
Power input connector. 5.5mm OD / 2.1mm ID is standard for 9-12V robot power supplies.
📎
HEADER PINS
2.54mm pitch
Male/female headers for connecting modules (Arduino shields, sensors). Essential for any robot PCB.
🔧
SCREW TERMINAL
3.5mm / 5.08mm
Clamp-style connector for motor wires and power cables. Much more secure than solder for high vibration.

Choosing the right connector is critical for reliable robotics. Wrong connectors = loose wires at the worst moment.

🟠
JST-XH / PH / SH
2.54 / 2.0 / 1.0mm
Keyed connectors with locking. JST-XH for battery/motors, JST-SH (Qwiic) for I²C sensors.
🔵
USB TYPE-C
SMD footprint
Power + data. Standard for modern robot controllers. 5V/3A capable with proper traces.
⬛
XT30 / XT60
TH, high current
LiPo battery connectors. XT30 = 30A, XT60 = 60A. Gold-plated contacts. Standard for drones.
🟢
QWIIC / STEMMA QT
JST-SH 4-pin
Standardized I²C connector. Daisy-chain sensors easily. No soldering for modules.

07 BEGINNER MISTAKES TO AVOID
NO DECOUPLING CAPS
ICs are sensitive to power supply noise. Without decoupling capacitors (0.1μF + 10μF) near every IC's power pin, your circuit will behave erratically or fail.
Place a 100nF ceramic cap within 3mm of every IC VCC pin, and a 10μF electrolytic per power zone.
TRACES TOO THIN FOR POWER
Running 2A through a 0.2mm trace will heat it up, increase resistance, cause voltage drops, and potentially burn the PCB — destroying your robot.
Use a trace width calculator. For 2A, use at least 0.8mm. Use wide polygon pours for heavy power.
NO GROUND PLANE
Single traces for ground create loop antennas that generate noise. This kills signal integrity, causes sensor errors, and is the #1 reason beginner boards behave strangely.
Always flood unused areas with a copper pour connected to GND on both layers.
WRONG FOOTPRINT
You designed with an SOT-23 footprint but your component is SOT-23-5 (different pin count). Board comes back and nothing fits — expensive mistake.
Always verify footprint vs datasheet 3D model. KiCad's 3D viewer is excellent for this. Order a sample before bulk.
NO TEST POINTS
Your board has a bug. You have no test points. You can't probe any signals with an oscilloscope or multimeter. Debugging becomes nearly impossible.
Add exposed copper test pads to every important signal — SPI clock, power rails, UART TX/RX.
POLARIZED PARTS BACKWARDS
Electrolytic capacitor or diode placed in reverse polarity. Best case: board doesn't work. Worst case: capacitor explodes, IC burns, board is destroyed instantly.
Always check polarity markers in schematic AND layout. Use clear silkscreen + and – labels.
